Introduction¶

This dataset represents appointment no-show data of 300,000 patients in Brazil. It conists of 15 features.
We are interested in analyzing the relations between a few features and no-shows by data visualization mainly, on both one and two dimensional levels.

Conclusions¶

There seem to be no difference between males and females in proportion of no-shows.

The percentage of no-show patient that did received and SMS is 44%, which might suggest that it is not a largley affecting feature.

Most appointments are on Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday, whereas the least amount of appointments is on Saturday. There are no appointments on Sunday which might suggest that the hospital does not operate on that day.

Most number of no-shows is on Tuesday and Wednesday. Saturday has the least amount of no-shows, but it is meaningless because Saturday accounts for 0.04% of total appointments.

Age distribution is skewed to the right, and I calculated the median instead of the mean to reduce the effect of any outliers. The median age for no-shows is 33, whereas it is 38 for shows.

Top neighbors in no-shows are plotted, but there aren't enough information to suggest why these neighbors score high in no-shows. One guess is that they are far from the hospital.

The data is collected over a short period of time, which is not enough to have an insight regarding cycles

Having a feature that has the distance to the hospital from each hospital might be a good idea to have better predictions.
